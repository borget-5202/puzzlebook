How to Play & Strategies

  • Use each of the 4 numbers exactly once to make 24.
  • Allowed: + − * / ^ (power) and parentheses.
  • Ranks: A=1, J=11, Q=12, K=13, T=10.
  • Shortcuts: Enter=Check, D=Deal, N=No solution, H=Help, Shift+H=Help all, S=Skip, R=Restart, X=Exit.

General

  1. Every game has 4 poker cards.
    – Number cards use their face value
    – A = 1, J = 11, Q = 12, K = 13
  2. Build your formula.
    – Use +, −, ×, ÷, and ^ (exponentiation).
    – Use parentheses ( ) to override normal precedence.
    – Use each card exactly once.

Tips & Strategies

❤️ Spot quick pairs:
– Look for two cards whose sum or product is a factor of 24.

❤️ Factor trees:
– Break 24 into convenient pieces (e.g. 24 = 6×4 or 8×3) and work backwards.

❤️ Exponent shortcuts:
– When you see a "2" and a "3," remember 2³ = 8—the exponent rule can unlock many combos.

❤️ Keep parentheses tight:
– Group complex sub-expressions first—this reinforces PEMDAS and prevents slip-ups.
